about sequence

welcome friends. sequence is a sample driven (but also midi!) step sequencer with a chord-relative harmonic engine. while you can treat it fully as a 'place steps on a grid' sequencer, it is also able to be harmonically aware and can shift your patterns beyond the initial placements. this doc helps run through the surface of what this can do — if you have any questions please don't hesitate to hit up @newspeechsound on instagram and i'll talk you through any issues!

also — please share whatever you make! i'm so curious about what this will turn into with your help.

— chris elkjar, july 2026

quickstart

from a blank app to sound:

  • click the — button at the left of any row and pick a voice. voices load from your samples folder (settings → user samples).
  • ⌘/ctrl-click steps to place notes. the same gesture removes them. a plain click only selects a step — it never places one.
  • press space.
  • toggle between rhythm and melody at the bottom of the rack — eight rows each.
  • set bpm, root and scale in the transport bar. change root or scale mid-play and everything reharmonizes instantly.
  • turn up a row's mutation dial and let it drift.

the grid

every gesture on a step:

⌘/ctrl click
toggle the step on/off — this is how notes are placed and removed.
click
select the step. selection pins it for the roll tab's inspector and for ↑/↓ editing, and sets the tie anchor.
shift click
tie — ties or unties the run of steps between the anchor and the clicked step (melodic rows).
drag ↑↓
edit the current mode's value on a lit step — pitch in notes mode (melodic rows), otherwise velocity, chance, ratchet, timing or gate.
shift drag
velocity, whatever mode you're in.
⌘/ctrl drag
chance, whatever mode you're in.
scroll
same edits as dragging — hover a step and scroll. the shift/⌘ overrides apply here too.

edit modes are the six buttons in the transport (keys 1–6): notes, velocity, chance, ratchet, timing, gate. with a step selected, ↑/↓ nudges its value in the current mode.

the grid always shows the pattern as you authored it. mutation, density fills and chance-misses happen in the audio — and in the roll tab's live overlay — without rewriting your steps.

rhythm + melody

drum and melodic, toggled at the bottom of the rack.

drum rows trigger one sound per step — kicks, snares, hats, percussion. eight rows.

melodic rows play pitched material. eight rows too, but they aren't equal — their behavior depends on where they sit in the stack.

positional roles

the first melodic row is the chord master. on each step you can pick a chord by degree (I–VII) and extension (triad, 7, 9, 11, sus2, sus4), with optional inversion and spread. when it fires, it publishes that chord to the rest of the melodic rows.

the second row is the bass. it sits two octaves down and plays one chord tone per step — the root by default, but you can author the 3rd, 5th, 7th into specific steps via the inspector.

rows three through eight are open. set them to follow the chord, drone on the root, walk the scale, or ignore the chord entirely. piano lines, counter-melodies, leads, pads, percussion — whatever the piece needs.

new melodic rows you add later default to ignore — they play their authored steps independently of the chord master. opt them into following via the row panel.

follow modes

each non-chord-master melodic row has an interp dropdown:

ignore
independent. step.pitch is a scale-degree offset from the scene root.
chord
step.pitch picks a tone from the chord master's chord. 0 = root, 1 = 3rd, 2 = 5th, 3 = 7th, and so on.
scale
diatonic walk above the chord root, in the scene scale.
drone
holds the chord master's root every step. authored pitch is ignored.

rows

left of each grid, per row: the voice picker, a row-settings button, a record-arm dot, six knobs — gain, pan, cutoff, res, fx send, mutation — then mute and solo. ⌘/ctrl-click mute to clear the whole row.

row settings holds the pattern math — len (1–64), euclidean hits, rotation, step rate — plus, on melodic rows, the default chord voicing (C / E / I / S), the interp follow mode and octave, and per-row output routing.

rows longer than sixteen steps grow page buttons above the grid. next to them sits a timing-lock dot: locked, mutation only changes notes and leaves the rhythm alone.

the screen

the panel at the top is tabbed: params · automation · roll · lfo · fx · master · perform · loops · noise. ` cycles through them. params, automation and roll follow the focused row; everything else is global.

params

the instrument editor for the focused row's voice. a waveform display with draggable start/end trim (wheel zooms, two-finger scroll pans), four playmodes — sample, slice, wavetable, granular — and the sound-shaping stack: volume / tune / finetune, reverb and delay sends, an lp/hp/bp filter, and a drive + bit-crush stage.

each playmode brings its own group: loop modes for sample, auto/grid slicing with a sensitivity knob for slice, window size + scan position for wavetable, grain shape/length/position/scatter for granular. in slice mode, double-click the waveform to add a marker, drag one to move it, alt/⌘-click to remove it, click a region to audition it.

edits apply to the instrument everywhere it's used. save / save as to keep them, revert to discard, export .pti to take the voice to a polyend tracker.

automation

per-instrument modulation. each target — volume, cutoff, pan, pitch, plus grain position or wavetable position when the playmode has one — gets an lfo or an envelope, one or the other. lfos have a shape, a tempo-synced division and a depth; envelopes are a draggable adsr curve plus depth.

roll

a piano-roll view of the focused row, plus the step inspector. while playing, the authored pattern dims and the notes actually sounding — after mutation, fills, chance — draw bright on top. this is where you watch the machine edit.

the inspector edits whichever step you last clicked: velocity / chance / ratchet / timing / gate readouts, per-step chord overrides (degree, extension, inversion, spread) on chord-capable rows, slice choice (or per-hit random) on sliced voices, and the accumulator — a per-step climbing ladder with its own step size, range and wrap / bounce / hold shape.

lfo

four global lfos, each with a depth knob and a shape button (sin · tri · saw · sqr). click a cell to select it, then click any bindable knob anywhere in the app — row knobs, fx, master, loops, noise, instrument sends — to route the lfo there; click again to unroute, esc to deselect. a routed knob shows a small L# tag and animates with the modulation; grabbing it by hand overrides the lfo.

fx

the global send-fx chain: a pre-drive, a tape stage (position, length, reverse, hold, grain rate/mix, mix), glitch (chance, mix), reverb (size, diffusion, damping — it's a fully wet return; the amount comes from the per-row and per-instrument sends), and a synced delay (time, feedback, ping-pong, lo-fi).

master

the output chain, with a preset dropdown at the left: input gain and lo-cut, a compressor (amount, attack, release), a distortion stage (mode, drive, bias, mix), then hi-cut, trim, an output gate and bypass.

perform

live punch-ins over whatever's running. hold a repeat pad for momentary beat-repeat; everything else latches on click — tune, filter, bits, scrub, chop, sat, verb, delay, plus a rev pad that plays new triggers as reverse one-shots. the track pads along the bottom choose which rows get punched (none selected = all of them). toggle set to drag new values into the slots; leave it off so a stray scroll can't rewrite your rig.

loops

a tape-loop / resampler unit. the capture buttons grab the last 1, 2, 4 or 8 bars of the mix — retroactively, bar-locked, seamless. the loop layer plays the buffer back with thru-zero speed (a pitch-lock toggle turns that into pure timestretch) while the granular layer chews the same buffer: pitch, grain size, voice count, spawn rate (synced or free), start-point randomness, its own level. shift-drag speed / pitch / size / rate to add per-grain deviation. sends to fx, reverb and delay; save bounces the loop's output into your samples library as a new voice.

noise

a digital-noise unit built around a wasp-style filter (drive, cutoff, resonance, stereo width, lp/bp taps). its input can be an insert on the loop unit, a parallel return, its own bar-quantized capture with vari-speed playback, or nothing at all — self-sounding clocked noise. the noise knob feeds noise into the audio path; cv feeds it into the cutoff. the clock can sync to the grid, run free, or derive from signal zero-crossings — its own input, the loop unit, or the whole mix — which is where the interesting cross-rhythms come from.

the mutation knob

every row has a mutation dial. it introduces controlled randomness within the row's authored material — velocity jitter, pitch jumps within an octave, gate-length wobble, ties flipping on and off.

on the chord master, mutation also does harmonic work: drop a tone from the chord, borrow a chord from the parallel mode (major ↔ minor), shuffle inversion, shift the spread. crank it and the band reharmonizes itself in real time.

macros

the strip at the top right. freeze holds the current state; auto hands the wheel to the ghost — it walks the scene/pattern graph on its own, morphs the macros between patterns and plays fills into transitions. the readout under it counts bars to its next move.

six knobs modulate the whole rig at once:

density
bipolar. below center, thins hits (offbeats drop first, downbeats survive); above, fills empty steps.
motion
bipolar harmonic motion — bar-aligned pitch jumps, size and direction from the knob.
drift
how often those motion jumps actually fire; also drifts pad voicings.
chaos
multiplies every row's mutation amount.
tension
biases mutation toward stable or colorful moves.
voicing
opens chords up — inversion, spread, extensions — never below what you authored.

craft a song

the pad rows above the rack. pattern pads snapshot and recall full rack states, bar-quantized — click to queue (swaps at the next boundary), shift-click to save the current state into a slot, ⌘/ctrl-click to clear, drag to reorder. scene pads work the same one level up — a scene holds a whole set of patterns plus its settings.

song opens the arrangement view: rows of scene + pattern + bar count, each with a per-track mute overlay, played top to bottom when engaged. song mode overrides the ghost's autonomous walking.

recording

arm rec in the transport; capture starts on play and keeps writing after stop until the reverb and delay tails ring out. with multi off you get one mixed wav; on, a full stems folder — master, fx, reverb and delay buses plus a dry wav per track. count adds a one-bar count-in; metro is a click that never reaches the recording. takes land in the recordings folder set in settings.

midi

settings → midi. pick a record-input port, arm a row (the dot next to its knobs), and play: with the transport running, notes overdub quantized under the playhead; stopped, hovering a step writes the note onto that step. incoming notes snap to the current scale.

the clock row makes sequence a clock master (24-ppqn out, one or two destinations) or a follower. mappings bind hardware ccs to any knob or pad via learn mode — or load the launch control xl3 preset for a full pre-built surface. a launchpad in programmer mode edits the grid directly.
